FCFS Scheduling Algorithm

FCFS (First-Come, First-Served) Scheduling Algorithm

FCFS is the simplest type of scheduling algorithm in which processes are executed based on their arrival time. The process that arrives first is executed first, followed by the next process in the queue. It operates on a non-preemptive basis, meaning once a process starts executing, it continues until it completes.

Characteristics of FCFS:

  • Non-preemptive: Once a process starts executing, it runs to completion.
  • Simple to implement.
  • May lead to the "convoy effect" where short processes get stuck behind long processes.
  • Does not consider the burst time of processes for scheduling.

Advantages:

  • Easy to understand and implement.
  • No starvation: Every process gets a chance to execute eventually.

Disadvantages:

  • Not suitable for environments where short processes need quick execution.
  • May result in poor average turnaround time, especially with varying burst times.
